Definitions

Anatomy

Anatomy is the branch of biology which is concerned with the study of the structure of organisms and their parts. Anatomy is a branch of the natural sciences that deals with the structural organization of living things.

Biomechanics

Biomechanics is the science of movement in a living body, including how muscles, bones, tendons and ligaments work together to move. Biomechanics is part of the broader field of kinesiology, focusing specifically on the mechanics of the movement.

Bloodletting

Bloodletting is the withdrawal of blood from a patient to prevent or cure illness and disease.

Buboes

Buboes are a symptom of bubonic plague and manifest as painful swelling of the thighs, neck, groin or armpits. They are caused by the bacteria Yersinia pestis spreading from flea bites through the bloodstream to the lymph nodes, where the bacteria replicate, causing the nodes to swell.

Capitalist Economy

Capitalism is an economic system in which individuals or companies own capital goods. The production of goods and services is based on supply and demand on the general market rather than on central planning.

Clergy

The clergy are an official leader within established religions. Their roles and functions vary according to religious traditions but generally involve presiding over specific rituals and teaching the doctrines and practices of their religion.

Coat of Arms

The coat of arms, the main part of a system of hereditary symbols dating back to the beginning of medieval Europe, was mainly used to establish identity in combat.

Commune

A commune is an intentional community of people living together, sharing common interests, often having common values and beliefs.

Crusaders

A crusader is a person who participated in one of the military expeditions undertaken by the Christian powers in the 11th, 12th and 13th centuries to gain the Holy Land for Muslims.

Dissect

To dissect something is to cut it methodically to study its internal parts.

Feudalism (Feudal Economy)

Feudalism was a combination of legal, economic, military and cultural customs that flourished in medieval Europe between the 9th and 15th centuries. Broadly defined, it was a means of structuring society around relationships derived from the possession of land in exchange for services or work.

Fief

A fief was the central element of feudalism. These were hereditary goods or rights granted by a suzerain to a vassal who held them in exchange for a form of feudal allegiance and service, generally given by personal ceremonies of homage and loyalty.

Guilds

A guild is an association of craftsmen or traders who oversee the practice of their craft/trade in a particular area. The first types of guilds consisted of brotherhoods of traders, normally operating in a single city and covering a single trade.

Immunities

In biology, immunity is the balanced state of multicellular organisms with adequate biological defences to fight infections, diseases or other undesirable biological invasions, while having an adequate tolerance to avoid allergies and autoimmune diseases.

Inheritance

An inheritance includes the assets of an estate that are bequeathed, in whole or in part, to specific heirs.

Kinsmen

A Kinsman is a man who is one of a person's blood relations.

Lords

The Lord is an appellation for a person or a deity who has authority, control or power over others, acting as a master, leader or leader.

Mercenary

A mercenary, sometimes called a soldier of fortune, is an individual who participates in a military conflict for personal gain, is otherwise a stranger to the conflict and is not a member of any other official army. Mercenaries fight for money or other forms of payment rather than for political interests.

Monarchy

A motte-and-bailey castle is a fortification with a wooden or stone dungeon located on a raised ground called a motte, accompanied by an enclosed courtyard, or bailey, surrounded by a protective ditch and a palisade.

Motte-and-bailey

A motte-and-bailey castle is a fortification with a wooden or stone dungeon located on a raised ground called a motte, accompanied by an enclosed courtyard, or bailey, surrounded by a protective ditch and a palisade.

Mysticism

Mysticism is the practice of religious ecstasies with all the ideologies, ethics, rites, myths, legends and magic that can be linked to it.

Naturalism

Naturalism in art refers to the representation of realistic objects in a natural setting. The realistic movement of the 19th century advocated naturalism in reaction to stylized and idealized representations of the subjects of Romanticism, but many painters have used a similar approach over the centuries.

Noble

A nobleman is someone who belongs to a hereditary class with a high social or political status.

Nomadic

Most nomadic groups follow a fixed annual or seasonal pattern of movement and settlement. Nomadic peoples traditionally travel by animal or canoe or on foot.

Occultist

An occultist is a person who believes in the supernatural and the power of magic.

Overlords

An overlord, or suzerain, in the English feudal system, was a lord of a manor who had given a manor, a succession or particular expenses, to a tenant.

Pandemic

A pandemic is an epidemic of disease that has spread in a large region, for example, several continents or in the world, affecting a significant number of people.

Peace of God

An exemption from an attack in the feudal war requested by the church from the end of the 9th century for all peoples and all consecrated places and later for all those who claimed the protection of the church.

Peasants

A peasant is a pre-industrial farm worker or farmer with limited land ownership, particularly a feudal resident of the Middle Ages and paying rent, taxes, fees or services to a landlord. In Europe, there were three classes of peasants: slaves, serfs and free tenants.

Physicians

A physician, a medical practitioner or quite simply a doctor is a professional who practices medicine, which aims to promote, maintain or restore health by the study, diagnosis, prognosis and treatment of diseases, injuries and other physical and mental disorders.

Pillaging

Looting, also referred to as sacking, ransacking, plundering, despoiling, despoliation, and pillaging, is the indiscriminate taking of property by force in the context of a military or political victory, or during a disaster, such as a war, a natural disaster or a riot.

Primogeniture

Primogeniture is the right, by law or custom, of the first legitimate son to inherit the whole or principal succession of his parents, in preference to the inheritance shared by all or certain children, any illegitimate child or any collateral parent.

Realism

ARealism in the arts is generally the attempt to represent a subject honestly, without artifice and by avoiding artistic conventions or implausible, exotic and supernatural elements.

Remuneration

Remuneration is compensation or other financial compensation provided in exchange for services rendered by an employee. Several additional benefits in addition to wages are increasingly popular compensation mechanisms. Compensation is an element of reward management.

Scapegoat

The scapegoat is the practice of distinguishing an individual or group from undeserved blame and the resulting negative treatment.

Siege

A siege is a military blockade of a city or a fortress, to conquer by attrition or a well-prepared assault. Siege warfare is a form of constant low-intensity conflict characterized by a strong and static defensive position.

Silk Road

The Silk Road was a network of trade routes connecting East and West and was at the heart of economic, cultural, political and religious interactions between these regions from the 2nd century BCE to the 18th century.

Squire

From the Middle Ages, a squire was wearing a shield or a knight's armour.

Subordinate

A subordinate is a lower level or an item classified at a lower level

Subservient

Dominance and submission, also called submission, are a set of behaviours, customs and rituals involving the submission of one person to another in an erotic episode or a way of life.

Vassals

A vassal is a person considered to have a mutual obligation towards a lord or a monarch, in the context of the feudal system of medieval Europe. The obligations often included military support of the knights in exchange for certain privileges, including land held as tenant or fief.

Wages

A wage is a monetary compensation paid by an employer to an employee in exchange for the work performed. Payment can be calculated as a fixed amount for each task completed, or at an hourly or daily rate, or based on an easily measurable amount of work.
